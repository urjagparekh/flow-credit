1. Problem

Almost every AI product today — image, video, text, audio — is metered by credits. On the commerce side, these platforms sell subscriptions, and each subscription is capped at a fixed number of credits per cycle. So one problem is common to all of them: a user can run out of credits at any moment, depending on how much they use.

It's a new problem, created by the rise of these products — and it lands hardest on good, loyal users doing real work. When they're interrupted mid-task by credit exhaustion, the only experience on offer today is a cold stop: "pay" or "top up." This project makes that moment a smart, per-user decision instead of a blunt cutoff.

2. Why it matters

  • Customer churn — you break their flow at the worst moment. The wall lands when the user is deep in the work: high intent, high emotion, and often on a clock — a YouTuber publishing to catch peak engagement, a freelancer on a client deadline. Forcing them to stop and sort out billing right then is exactly when frustration peaks and good users walk away.
  • One blunt rule for everyone — your best customers feel it most. The cutoff has no idea who's at the gate. It treats a loyal, three-year, always-paid subscriber exactly like a brand-new or fraudulent account — same cold wall, same friction. The experience never reflects the relationship you've earned, so your most valuable users get punished right alongside the bad actors.
  • Lost revenue — a slammed door instead of an open one. At that moment a user's willingness to pay is at its peak; many would gladly top up to keep going. A hard "upgrade to continue using" or "credits used, come back later" leaves that money on the table instead of offering a smooth way forward — and the same blunt path that loses good sales also waves risky accounts straight through.

3. Who struggles

Primary: solopreneurs, content creators, YouTubers, and freelancers who rely on these tools for time-sensitive, business-critical work — usually deep in a creative session when the credits run out.

Secondary: B2B expansion for enterprises (future scope).

4. How it helps

  • Keeps trusted users in flow instead of cutting them off — reducing churn at the highest-intent moment.
  • Makes the right call per user — continue, warn, offer to buy, or stop — rather than one wall for everyone.
  • Protects margin and stops risky accounts; it doesn't extend credit blindly.
  • Captures revenue from users who would happily keep paying.
  • Frees the support team from manual judgment calls, escalating only the genuinely hard cases.

5. Goal & where it scales

Goal: when a user is about to run out mid-generation, decide — in that moment, per user — the right way to handle it (keep creating, ask for payment, limit future use, or stop), and explain why.

Where it scales: the same judgment extends naturally to B2B enterprise plans with shared credit pools and many seats — bringing context-aware overage decisions and fair-usage guardrails to teams.

6. How it works

Safety checks (rules) → Judgment (the agent) → Final cap check → Decision
  • Safety checks first, in plain code: fraud, failed payments, consent, hard limits. Clear-cut cases are settled here.
  • Judgment in the grey zone, by the agent: for everything else, it weighs the signals that matter — how valuable and trusted the user is, the size of the request, their payment history — and reasons through the trade-offs.
  • Final check: the decision is clamped to the platform's limits before anything happens.

The agent investigates only as much as each case needs — a simple case stays quick; a tricky one digs deeper.

7. How to interact

  • Pick a user scenario from the list below.
  • Press Generate — the user is about to run a job that costs more credits than they have left.
  • The agent responds with its decision, the reasons behind it, the alternatives it rejected, and the impact on the next bill.
  • A data view shows the underlying account details the agent reasoned over.

9. Architecture

End-to-end flow. Deterministic rules (slate) guard the decision on both sides; the agent (teal) reasons only in the middle.

10. The mechanics

The key numbers the agent works with (all illustrative):

ItemValue
Price per credit (charged)$0.10
Cost per credit (to serve)$0.04  →  $0.06 margin
Continuity cap per cycleStandard 50 · Pro 200 · Team 400 credits

Confirm required above$5 of grant value
Auto-block thresholdsfraud score ≥ 70, or ≥ 2 failed payments (90 days)

How they combine: the safety checks (fraud, failed payments, consent) run first. If none stop the request, the agent may grant up to the smaller of the overage, the remaining continuity cap, or 150 credits — and if the grant is worth more than $5, it asks for confirmation before charging.

11. What changed

A hard, one-size-fits-all cutoff becomes a real-time, per-user judgment — the moment that used to push good customers away now keeps them creating, while still protecting the platform.
